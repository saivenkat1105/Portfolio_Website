This poem was written for an inter-hostel competition under the theme 'Argonauts'. Argonauts were a group of Greek heroes who accompanied Jason onboard the ship Argo on a quest to retrieve the Golden Fleece. During their journey, they first stopped at Lemnos, an island inhabited only by women. This poem describes the story of these Women of Lemnos. You can find more information about the Argonauts [here](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Argonauts).

***

*Big and small, tall and short,*  
*Weak and strong, fair and swart,*  
*Every man was given the chance,*  
*To give their wife one last glance.*  

*One after another we killed them all,*  
*Placed them under a velvet pall.*  
*Every wife killed her man,*  
*Our life as widows now began.*  

*We didn’t leave a single man alive,*  
*Everyone’s death had to arrive,*  
*For the philandering they have committed,*  
*This punishment was long awaited.*  

*We started ruling the isle,*  
*Decided to keep all men in exile.*  
*Years passed without a man in sight,*  
*Our hearts were now longing for love every night.*  

*Sadness engulfed us whole,*  
*Nothing happy was left in our soul.*  
*As despair ran through all our veins,*  
*There came a flicker of hope to reduce our pains.*  

*Miles ahead, we saw a ship,*  
*Looking from the isle, it was just a blip.*  
*As the ship came nearer and nearer,*  
*Our desires became stronger and stronger.*  

*All our dreams of unquenched love,*  
*Were about to get satisfied now.*  
*The ship now stopped at our shore,*  
*With the most handsome men at the oar.*  

*As all of them got onto their feet,*  
*Our hearts now skipped a beat.*  
*We all found our princes,*  
*They found their princesses.*  

*We were showered with love like never before,*  
*We couldn’t ask for anything more.*  
*We thought this would go forever,*  
*And this could end never ever.*  

*But the time had come for them to leave,*  
*Shocked we were, we forgot to grieve.*  
*They had a fleece to find,*  
*And a kingdom to mind.*  

*They can’t wait anymore here,*  
*This is what we dreaded to hear.*  
*With heavy hearts and heavier souls,*  
*We let them reach their goals.*  

*Our loveless life started again,*  
*With only their memories to increase the pain.*  
*Argonauts they called themselves,*  
*Unrequited lovers we called ourselves.*
