## 1. Less is More

I will start this tutorial with the most common mistake that everyone makes the first time they wash the dishes. They take all the dishes, apply soap and scrub all of them and then wash them all together. Most of you are probably doing this even now. But that’s not the right way. This method might work for glass or fiber plates but not for Indian steel utensils.

To explain why this is not the case, let me take an example. 

Imagine you were Ajit Pawar after the Maharashtra elections in 2019. You thought you had the support of all the NCP MLAs after talking to them one by one and happily switched over to BJP, took oath as Deputy CM in a secret ceremony to leave everyone in shock. But what happened? You later realized you didn’t actually have the support of them but they were cleverly deceiving you under the guidance of Mr. Sharad Pawar.

That’s what happens when you clean everything in one go. You think they look clean but after sometime they start deceiving you. When you apply soap on a utensil and leave it for some time, the water starts drying up and you are left with dry soap particles all over the plate. When you apply water, it seems as if they are gone but once it dries up again, you see the marks the dried soap particles leave you with. So don’t be like Ajit Pawar. Don’t be greedy. Take only a few utensils, scrub them with soap and clean them with water. Then take care of the next batch of utensils.

## 3. Prioritize your Utensils

This tip is for those who have a dish rack. I guess most Indian households use this.

![Dish Rack](/blog/dish_rack.avif)

What some people do is clean all the bigger utensils first and then do the smaller ones. The problem with this approach is when you place the bigger utensils in the rack, they occupy a lot of empty space. This is just like our PM’s COVID announcements and the economic package. They seem long and huge but except for occupying space in the headlines they amount to nothing. When you reach the end of the utensils, you might find yourself with no place for the smaller ones.

Hence, to use the limited space in these dish racks effectively, clean all the plates and small utensils first and then start cleaning the bigger ones. This way, all the utensils can be safely packed into the dish rack.
