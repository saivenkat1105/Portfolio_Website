If I had to describe my life at IIT Madras with a single word, it would be **Anveshak**. Joining the team was one of the best decisions I ever made, both for me and my resume. My journey with the team has been really incredible with loads of memories, inside jokes, foosball, robotics and whatnot.

## Who is Team Anveshak?

We are a group of 20+ undergraduate students who have a strong interest in robotics. We are on a mission to build the next generation of Mars Rovers that could aid astronauts in space one day. We participate in an international competition - **University Rover Challenge**, conducted by the Mars Society. It is a competition aimed at university students to encourage them to pursue robotics for space exploration. (I think I told this same intro to easily around 300 people on different occasions). We work in the Centre for Innovation (CFI), a place dedicated for teams like us by IIT Madras.

## My Journey

### Team Member (Nov 2017 – Jun 2018)

After a 3-month process of tests and mini projects, I got into the team. As a fresher, my work was basically buying stuff from outside, machining and assembling parts, tightening bolts, and — most importantly — filing the parts that didn't fit. It was painful, but it gave me a real understanding of design-for-manufacturability: the kind of problems you only discover when your hands are covered in metal shavings.

### Chassis Design Team — (Jun 2018 – Jun 2019)

This was the year I was actually working on the design of our rover, which we named **Caesar**. I designed the **rocker bogie suspension system** of the rover. This was basically to enable the rover go over rough terrain easily.

![Caesar](/blog/Caesar.avif)
![Rocker Bogie Suspension System](/blog/Rocker_Bogie.avif)

I was also part of the team that developed a completely autonomous **soil collection and testing laboratory** to be used onboard. And I handled most of the manufacturing to get the rover completed in time for competition.

**Results:**
-  Won the **Indian Rover Challenge (IRC) 2019** in our very first attempt
-  Placed **12th internationally** at the **University Rover Challenge 2019** out of 90+ teams

### Team Lead -  (Jul 2019 – May 2020)

![Team Anveshak at IRC 2020](/blog/Team_Photo.avif)

After returning from URC 2019, we started work on our next rover almost immediately. I became the **Chassis Design Lead** and **Finance Lead**, managing a budget of INR 7.85 lakhs. The rover was named **Dark Knight** (I wanted it to be *Dora the Explorer*, but my teammates weren't creative enough).

Managing the team, handling differences of opinion, and figuring out the path forward were things I genuinely grew into during this phase. Ideation, discussions, and endless deliberations went into every design decision.

Then COVID-19 happened and cancelled everything.

**Results:**
- Placed **5th** at IRC 2020 — a below-par performance due to some critical competition errors
- Placed **8th** after the qualification phase of URC 2020 (report + video submission)

## The Competitions

### IRC 2019 - *The Underdogs*

![Team Anveshak at IRC 2019](/blog/Team_Photo_2019.avif)

After a disastrous performance in URC 2018, we decided to participate in IRC 2019 so that we would be well prepared for URC 2019. IRC 2019 was held in Manipal Institute of Technology, Manipal. We barely managed to finish our rover before the competition and went into it with zero expectations owing to our lack of preparation and presence of top ranked foreign teams in the competition. None of us expected to return as the winners of the competition. 


This trip was definitely one of my most memorable experiences. We reached Manipal and started assembling and testing our rover. Seeing the rover run for the first time was a great moment. All the hard work we put in for the last few months seemed to pay off. Then the competition began. We performed decently on the first 2 days with a fair share of mistakes. We got to know that we were actually leading by the end of the 2nd day. But the science task on Day 3 was our weak spot, and we knew we had to perform well in that to stay ahead. As luck would have it, we made a complete mess out of it and we were pretty sure we had lost our chance. 


The closing ceremony was full of nervous chatter, anxious looks and tensed faces. They announced 2nd and 3rd places. That's when I started panicking. What if we weren't even in the top 3? How bad did we do in the last task? Like a classic anchor, the guy announcing the results was increasing the suspense. He gave a long pause, asked the audience who they thought the winner was, gave another long pause just to mess around with us. Then he announced our names and everyone of us literally jumped from our seats shouting and it was surreal. That moment when everyone jumped out of our chairs is a memory that I will forever treasure.


The rest of the day was just us wondering how we did it. Before the competition, we were all so confident that we will fail that we were telling each other that we shouldn't get demotivated by the end of it but now everything changed. We went for dinner outside and returned to drunk Manipal students doing weird shit in the Kamath Circle(A spot for students to hang out in Manipal University). We went on a trip the next day going around Manipal and just like that our 15 days of fun was over.

### URC 2019 - *Missed Opportunities*

![Team Anveshak at URC 2019](/blog/Team_Photo_URC_2019.avif)

After IRC 2019, we started preparing for URC 2019 and frankly, I think we got a little overconfident with our success at IRC. We were complacent and that affected our performance in URC 2019. The competition was held in Utah,USA and it was my first time going out of India.


We started from Mumbai, reached Salt Lake City (SLC), assembled and tested our rover. We then went to Hanksville, the place where the competition was going to happen, and we continued testing. The competition started. We again did decently well the first 2 days with its ups and downs. The 3rd day's task was again our weak spot, and we again faltered in it; this time the results were disastrous. We failed to place in the top 10 and were **12th**, losing out to the 11th-placed team by about 0.3 points. Everyone was pretty disappointed that day and we packed our bags to return to Salt Lake City.

We toured around SLC the next day and it was really beautiful. The city was calm; we trekked to the top of Ensign Peak in SLC and the view was breathtaking. We soon forgot our earlier disappointment and enjoyed the views the USA was offering us. We then toured Los Angeles and Las Vegas before coming back to India. We did some crazy stuff there and had one of our biggest adventures and incidents which none of us can ever forget.

### IRC 2020 — *The Comeback That Wasn't*

This year we were hoping to repeat our last year's performance and put in a considerable effort into manufacturing the rover on time and we were able to do a lot of testing as well. This time, the competition was in Vellore Institute of Technology, Chennai. The hospitality was horrible and we had to travel daily from our college to the competition site in buses, going early in the morning and returning in the night.


The competition began and we did extremely well on the first day and we were really happy. Then disaster struck us. The 2nd task was harder than expected and we didn't perform up to the mark and during the setup of the 3rd task, we blew up our motherboard to return empty handed. Contrary to our previous competitions, the 3rd day went well and in the end we were placed 5th.

We realized we had to improve a lot and started working towards URC 2020 when Corona took over the world and stopped everything.

## My Experiences

Some of the fondest memories I have of IIT Madras will mostly have Team Anveshak linked to it. My daily routine was to wake up, go to classes and end up in CFI (the place where we work) by evening only to leave at midnight and then go to Ramu to have our midnight Maggi and egg sandwich. I probably spent more time in CFI than in my room. There were days when I never went to my room for 3-4 days just staying in CFI as deadlines approached.  We were either working, playing foosball or debating on issues ranging from Ram Mandir to Republicans, Statue of Unity to Statue of Liberty, Gandhi to Game of Thrones. We watched movies, IPL finals and GOT episodes right there. When our institute didn't provide us accommodation due to a water crisis, I made CFI my sleeping place for 2 weeks. 


But not everything was fun. The pain of manufacturing the rover is beyond words. Buying raw material from shops in Chennai with vendors who speak only Tamil, giving it for manufacturing in a different shop, paining that guy to complete our parts(Again, they speak only Tamil) and running around our Central Workshop was a routine. These trips to buy stuff were called joyrides but there was literally no joy in them. I had to undergo joyrides from Adyar to either Ekkatuthangal, Parry's, Ambatthur, Royapettah, Mount Road in the Chennai buses (Again, the conductors speak only Tamil) and local trains in the scorching heats only to be sent back empty handed to come again the next day.Special mention to JNS Metals and Sree Balamurugan Engineering Enterprises(SBEE) both in Ekkatuthangal for making our joyrides hell. 


All in all, I cannot imagine what I would have done in my insti life if I wasn't in Team Anveshak. The main reason I tried to join the team back then was because I thought the US trip was free and it was a big motivational factor. In the end, the trip wasn't free but I have no regrets. The technical knowledge I gained from the team was unparalleled and no amount of books can give the practical aspects of engineering that I understood from building the rover. 3 years at Anveshak was definitely a roller coaster ride - a ride which if possible, I would like to take again and again and again.
